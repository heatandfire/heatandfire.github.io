Hosein Sadeghi
Hosein joined the group as a PhD student in June 2022, studying multiphysics simulations of Lithium-ion battery fires. His background is in Mechanical Engineering, Thermofluids. Prior to joining KCL, he was a research assistant at the fire safety engineering group of Aalto University funded by Academy of Finland and the Finnish Fire Protection Fund. His research focused on radiative properties of the fuel vapors in fires and he graduated from Aalto University with his Licentiate degree in 2022. In his free time, Hosein enjoys playing Iranian traditional music. He shares some of his videos on his Youtube Channel
Marissa Dauner
Marissa is a PhD student who joined the lab in October 2025. She is studying wildfire spread for development of a physics-based global use model. Marissa graduated from the University of Colorado, Boulder in 2025 with her MS in Mechanical Engineering and received her BS from Marquette University in 2022. Her previous graduate research focused on VOC emissions monitoring of prescribed fires in the U.S. Her PhD is funded by the ERC FIREMOD project.
Francesca Lugaresi
Francesca joined the group as a Postdoc in November 2023, working on the Faraday Institution SAFEBATT project focusing on battery fire spread modelling. Her background is in both Civil Engineering and Mechanical Engineering: She carried out a BEng Civil Engineering degree at the University of Edinburgh, an MSc at Imperial College, and her PhD in Mechanical Engineering at Imperial College London.
Ally Mamgain
Ally joined the group as a part-time PhD student in June 2023, studying the impacts of lithium-ion battery fires in a confined subterranean environment. She obtained her MSc degree in Chemical Process Engineering from UCL in 2017, where her thesis focused on the multi-criteria optimisation of emergency isolation valves on transportation pipelines and modelled the consequences of pipeline rupture. She has since worked as a Fire Safety Engineer primarily involved in the rail sector, supporting the design of stations with respect to fire from a life safety aspect. In her free time, Ally enjoys playing badminton and currently plays in the District league.
Abdullah Rehman
Abdullah joined the group as a PhD student in October 2024, studying the impact of wildfires on wildland urban interfaces and urban infrastructure as part of the Leverhulme Centre for Wildfires, Environment and Society. He obtained an MEng in Biomedical Engineering from Imperial College London in 2022, where he specialised in computational methods and data analysis. He has since worked as a software engineer developing digital twins and surgical simulations. In his free time, Abdullah enjoys hiking and going for a run.
Elena Funk
Elena joined the group as a PhD student in October 2024. Her research focuses on developing methodology for battery design fires. Elena's recent academic journey began with a Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering from Örebro University in Sweden. She then pursued an International Master of Fire Safety Engineering (IMFSE) from Ghent University and Lund University. Elena is undertaking a joint industrial PhD with the Danish Fire Safety and Security Technology Institute (DBI). Elena also has a Master’s degree in Psychology from Russia and has been working in industry for 12 years prior to her recent studies. In her free time, she enjoys calisthenics, cooking, foraging, and hiking.
