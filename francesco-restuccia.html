Bio

Dr Francesco Restuccia is an Associate Professor in the Department of Engineering at King's College London. He comes from Italy, and obtained his MEng Degree in Electrical and Mechanical Engineering from the University of Edinburgh, MS in Mechanical Engineering from Caltech, and PhD in Mechanical Engineering at Imperial College London. Francesco's research currently focuses on transient heat transfer processes in electrochemical storage, vehicle waste-heat recovery, and safety hazards in low-carbon energy industries with a focus on ignition and fire spread.

Mechanics for Engineers
The aim of this module is to provide students with a fundamental knowledge of classical mechanics, including elements from both dynamics and statics. Topics will include: applications of Newton’s Laws, single particle & rigid body dynamics, impact, static equilibrium, vibration, shear and stress. Students will learn how to solve problems in mechanics, for example through drawing force diagrams and resolving forces, and applying Newton’s Laws.
Heat and Mass Transfer
This module builds up on the fundamental knowledge gained in 1st year Energy and 2nd year Thermofluids modules together with materials and develops an understanding of applied thermal sciences by exploring heat and mass transfer which is essential to improve energy efficiency of engineering solutions including thermal management of electronic systems and electronic doping.
Keys to Engineering
In this module students will take on projects designed to introduce both the practicalities of engineering and the project-based learning approach to study. Innovation in engineering is built on design. This module will guide students through the design thinking process, from understanding the user to defining the problem. Recognising that design is not just a technical, but also a creative, process, not just a skill, but also a discipline, in this module, students will both study design tools and explore their own creativity and learn how to work in a team.
Computational and Mathematical Thinking for Engineers
The Computational and Mathematical Thinking in Engineering module will provide students with core background and fundamental knowledge of mathematical and computational tools that will be used for the rest of the BEng. It will take a teaching approach in which new mathematical concepts are introduced and then demonstrated through exercises that motivate learning new programming concepts. The emphasis is very much on hands-on learning.
